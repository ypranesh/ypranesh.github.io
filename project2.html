01 · Business Problem

The Question That
Prompted This Analysis

"What's working, what isn't, and where should I focus?"

— Retail Manager, US Superstore

Without a centralised view, performance monitoring was scattered across spreadsheets, regional reports and product lists — making it impossible to spot trends, compare segments or identify which products were actually profitable.

📊
No single source of truth. Revenue, profit, region and segment data lived in separate reports with no way to cross-reference.
💸
Discount policy blind spot. No visibility into whether discounting was driving volume at the cost of margin — or destroying it entirely.
🔄
No retention visibility. Unknown what proportion of revenue came from first-time vs returning customers — and whether that ratio was improving.
🗺️
Geographic blind spots. Regional managers had no quick way to compare state-level profitability and identify where margins were being eroded.

03 · LOD Expressions

Four FIXED LOD
Expressions Used

Orders Per Customer
{ FIXED [Customer ID] : COUNTD([Order ID]) }
Calculates order count per customer independent of whatever dimensions are in the view. Powers the purchase frequency distribution chart.
Customer First Order Date
{ FIXED [Customer ID] : MIN([Order Date]) }
Tags every single order row with that customer's first ever purchase date — enabling the New vs Repeat Revenue split by quarter.
Avg Discount by Sub-Category
{ FIXED [Sub-Category] : AVG([Discount]) }
Pins average discount at Sub-Category level regardless of other view dimensions — ensures scatter plot accuracy without distortion.
Discount Band
{ FIXED [Order ID] : MAX([Discount]) }
Evaluates discount at Order ID level to avoid row-level distortion from dimension context. Used to bucket orders into No / Low / Medium / High discount bands.
04 · Key Findings

Four Business
Insights Uncovered

-$18K
Tables are deeply loss-making despite high sales
Tables rank among the top sub-categories by revenue but show a consistently negative profit ratio. The culprit is aggressive discounting — the scatter plot shows Tables sitting in the bottom-right quadrant: high discount, deeply negative margin.
Texas
Strong sales states can hide poor profitability
Texas and Illinois generate high sales volumes but show negative profit ratios on the map — driven by excessive regional discounting. The map using Profit Ratio instead of raw Sales makes this immediately visible in a way a standard map never would.
~1 in 3
Most customers only ever buy once
The frequency distribution shows the vast majority of customers in the "1 Order" bucket. The New vs Repeat chart confirms revenue is almost entirely acquisition-dependent with minimal repeat purchase behaviour — a structural retention problem.
41%+
High discounts systematically destroy margin
Sub-categories receiving discounts above 40% are uniformly loss-making. The trend line on the scatter plot confirms the linear relationship between discount rate and profit ratio destruction — every 10pp increase in discount corresponds to a measurable margin decline.
