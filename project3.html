01 · Business Problem

The Monday Morning
Problem

"We have no automated way to monitor weekly performance — reports are manual, slow, and we have no forward visibility."

— Operations Manager, UK Online Retailer

Every Monday morning the operations team manually pulled last week's sales data into a spreadsheet and emailed it to the team. The process took 2 hours, was prone to human error, and provided no forward-looking visibility into demand.

⏱️
2 hours every Monday. Manual data extraction, formatting and distribution consumed the start of every working week.
❌
Error-prone process. Manual copy-paste from multiple sources meant inconsistencies and mistakes went unnoticed week to week.
🔭
No forward visibility. Reports only showed what happened last week — no way to anticipate demand shifts or plan stock levels.
📄
No consistent format. Reports looked different every week depending on who made them — no standardised KPIs or layout.
02 · Pipeline

Five-Phase Automated
Pipeline

01
Data Cleaning & EDA
Pandas · Null Removal · Feature Engineering
▼

Loads both sheets from the Online Retail II Excel file and concatenates into a single dataframe. Removes cancellations (invoices prefixed 'C'), missing Customer IDs (23% of rows — guest checkouts), negative quantities and zero-price rows. Retains 805,549 clean rows — 75% of raw data.

🔧 Feature engineering: Revenue per row, ISO year-week label, year, month, day of week — all computed in a single pass.
02
Weekly Summary Metrics
GroupBy · AOV · Top Products · Top Countries
▼

Aggregates all orders by ISO year-week to produce weekly Revenue, Order Count, Unique Customers, Items Sold and Average Order Value. Also computes Top 10 products by revenue and Top 10 international markets excluding the UK (which alone accounts for 83% of revenue).

📊 Week-on-week comparison: Each metric automatically compared to the prior week with direction indicators (+/-) for the report.
03
Demand Forecasting
Exponential Smoothing · 8-Week Horizon · Trend Model
▼

Fits a Holt's linear trend Exponential Smoothing model to 104 weeks of clean weekly revenue data. The final 2 weeks are trimmed to avoid partial-week distortion at the dataset boundary. Projects 8 weeks of forward revenue with optimised parameters.

📝 Model note: Seasonal variants were tested but required 2 full cycles minimum. Trend-only model selected as the most statistically defensible approach for this dataset.
04
Automated PDF Report
FPDF2 · Matplotlib · Auto-Generated Narrative
▼

Generates 3 charts sized for PDF layout, then assembles a professional 2-page PDF. Every element — KPIs, charts, tables, captions and the narrative summary — is computed fresh from the data on each run. No manual editing ever required.

✍️ Auto-narrative: Plain English summary generated programmatically — "In 2011-W48, total revenue was £251,788, up 0.9% week on week..."
05
Scheduling & Automation
Windows Task Scheduler · Zero Human Involvement
▼

The script is configured in Windows Task Scheduler to run every Monday at 8am. The report is saved to a defined output path automatically with the week number in the filename — no human involvement required after initial setup.

⚙️ Result: A 2-hour manual Monday process replaced by a single scheduled script. Zero ongoing effort.

04 · Key Findings

What the Data
Revealed

83%
UK accounts for 83% of all revenue
£14.7M of £17.7M total revenue comes from the UK. Ireland, Netherlands and Germany are the only meaningful international markets at £622K, £554K and £431K respectively. International diversification is a significant untapped opportunity.
Thursday
Peak trading day — Sunday is dead
Order volume peaks on Thursday and drops sharply over the weekend — consistent with a B2B wholesale business where purchasing follows office working patterns. Marketing and promotions should concentrate Tuesday–Thursday.
£480
High AOV confirms wholesale profile
At £480 average order value this is clearly a wholesale operation. Weekly AOV is highly volatile — individual weeks can be 3–4x the average due to large institutional orders, making the 4-week moving average essential context.
£258K
8-week forecast projects stable revenue
The Exponential Smoothing model projects average weekly revenue of approximately £258,788 over the next 8 weeks — a stable continuation of the upward trend visible in the decomposition analysis. No dramatic seasonal dip expected in the near term.

1
Download the Online Retail II dataset from UCI Machine Learning Repository. Two-sheet Excel file.
2
Install dependencies: pip install pandas openpyxl matplotlib statsmodels fpdf2
3
Update DATA_PATH and OUTPUT_PATH at the top of weekly_report.py to your local paths.
4
Run: python weekly_report.py — PDF saved to output path automatically.
5
To schedule: open Task Scheduler, create weekly task on Monday 8am, set program to your Python executable and argument to weekly_report.py.

Tested on Python 3.8+ with Anaconda. Development notebook included for step-by-step exploration.
